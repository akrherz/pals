Convergence/Divergence Map Help Page

Convergence or divergence is an accumulation or removal of air at a point. If convergence dominates, air will accumulate, increasing the mass and cause the surface pressure to rise. This leads to the development of a high pressure system. If divergence dominates, air will leave the column thereby causing the mass to decrease and the low pressure can deepen. An indicator of convergence and divergence is the direction change of height lines in the upper atmosphere. Height lines at the 200 mb level that come together is a signal of convergence. If height lines at the 200 mb level are spreading apart, air molecules do not build up and divergence is implied.
