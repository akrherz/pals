SEVERE WEATHER INDICES
There are many severe weather indices that can be found on an upperair sounding that meteorologists look at to determine if severe weather will occur, or what type of severe weather will occur. Many of these indices look at the difference between temperatures and dew point temperatures at various levels of the atmosphere to determine their values. Here are a few you may see on a skew-t plot.

LIFTED INDEX (LI)
Lifted index values < 0 indicate possible buoyant convection.
LI < 0 Significant cumulus convection
LI 0 to -4 Showers
LI -4 to -6 Thunderstorms
LI < -6 Severe thunderstorms


K-INDEX

Listed is the K-Index value followed by the thunderstorm probability.
< 15, 0%
15-20, < 20%
21-25, 20-40%
26-30, 40-60%
31-35, 60-80%
36-40, 80-90%
> 40, ~100%


SWEAT

Severe WEAther Threat
> 300 Severe thunderstorms are likely
> 400 Tornadic thunderstorms are likely


TOTAL TOTALS (TT)

50-55 Severe weather threat
> 55 Tornado threat

BULK RICHARDSON NUMBER (BRN OR BRCH)

< 10 Shear is too strong
15-35 Best for severe weather
> 50 Multicell thunderstorms
