Forecast Exercise Summaries

Summer Cases

June 18, 1998
A derecheo occurred in IA, MO, and IL. Good radar for IA and MO.

June 27, 1998
Tornadoes and flooding occurred in the uppper Midwest and Ohio Valley regions.

June 28, 1998
A strong cyclone in the upper Midwest pushes east causing flooding in KS, MO, IL, IN, OH, WV, PA, and NY. No radar is available for this day.

June 30, 1998
A large cyclone is centered north of New York state causing severe weather along the east coast with tornadoes in PA, NY, and MD, along with flooding in the Northeast.

May 3, 1999
Violent tornadoes in Oklahoma including an F5 in Oklahoma City. Good radar and reflectivity for Oklahoma City.

May 16, 1999
Severe weather from Texas to Wisconsin.

May 17, 1999
Squall line in Texas along with flash flooding in TX, OK, AR, and IL.

June 1, 1999
Squall line in IL, tornadoes and flooding in OK, MO, AR, and IL.

June 2, 1999
Montana was the brunt of the severe weather today with tornadoes and flash flooding.

June 4, 1999
Large squall line from IA to IL with severe reports from WY to FL.

June 5, 1999
Strong cold front in western Nebraska bringing 30s to Colorado. Training echoes from SD to WI with tornadoes from WY to WI. Newspaper article appears on this day.

June 9, 1999
Severe weather from Colorado to West Virginia due to a cold front in the Midwest and one on the east coast.

June 10, 1999
Cold/stationairy front from WI to CO bringing severe weather from Colorado to New York. A satellite image of an MCC in the Midwest is avaiable.
June 27, 1999
Strong storms in MO due to a stationary front in the Midwest.

June 30, 1999
A cold front streches from Toronto to Texas producing storms out ahead of it. Good radar of tornado producing supercells in Kansas and Nebraska.

July 2, 1999
A warm front extends from NE through IA and into AL focusing storms ahead of it.

July 8, 1999
A strong cyclone is centered in the Dakotas. In the afternoon a squall line develops in NE and northwest IA. Tornadoes occurred in Minnesota. There is data available for satellite derived CAPE and helicity along with upperair data, profiler data, lightning, and satellite.

February 13, 2000
The Southeast was the target for severe weather today as numeruous reports of tornadoes and severe thunderstorms piled up over the region.

March 8, 2000
Unseasonably warm air from the Gulf all the way into the central Plains coupled with a cold front has caused large squall line from Texas to Nebraska.

March 23, 2000
Surface cyclone in Texas, possible MCS in TX/OK/KS producing storms from Texas to Nebraska and later into North Dakota and Minnesota. A squall line devlops in eastern OK, KS, and NE.

March 28, 2000
Low pressure develops in Texas with a dryline in west central Texas coupled with a cold front pushing down from the north sets off storms in central Texas.

April 16, 2000
Surface low in northern Missouri with strong storms near Des Moines, IA. A squall line develops ahead of a cold front from IL to AR with severe weather from IL to VA.

April 19, 2000
Models did a poor job during the day in Iowa. But a late night squall line develops over eastern Iowa and western Illinois.

April 20, 2000
The upper air maps show that the surface low from the previous day has gained strength during the night. The low moves out of the Midwest and into the Ohio River Valley where a strong line of thunderstorms form a squall line along the cold front.

May 11, 2000
A strong surface low pushes northeast from Kansas into central/eastern Iowa spawning multiple tornadoes near Waterloo, IA. Good radar and newspaper article.

May 17, 2000
A slow moving surface low moves into central Nebraska firing storms at the triple point in central and northeastern Nebraska.

May 18, 2000
Low pressure moves slowly into southcentral Iowa producing severe storms along warm front near I-80 around 11 am with tornadoes. As the system slowly moves east, a large squall line develops from Texas to Wisconsin with rotating storms in northern Illinois. Extremely large hail reported just north of Chicago.

May 30, 2000
A low pressure system moves out of the western Plains and into the Midwest where a stationary front develops in Nebraska and Iowa. A low level jet out of the southwest draws up temperatures in the 90`s into a region where dew points are already in the 70`s. This helps to trigger thunderstorms and supercell thunderstorms in Iowa.

June 3, 2000
An area of lower pressure moves out of the Plains and into the Midwest causing thunderstorms and supercell thunderstorms to break out. Also a stationary front in Texas lights off some thunderstorms as well.

June 11, 2000
A late afternoon and early evening breakout of tornadic thunderstorms occurs from Texas to North Dakota. A second area of severe weather occurs on a stationary front in the Northeast.

June 12, 2000
A surface low in southern Canada has a stationary front that stretches from the northern Plains through the Ohio Valley and into the Northeast. Strong temperature and dew point gradients will help set off storms along the frontal boundary and in the Midwest.

June 13, 2000
An excellent squall line event. A low pressure system in the upper Midwest interacts with a warm, moist air mass. A strong squall line develops on the cold front as it moves east through MN to KS.

June 15, 2000
An extensive cold front in the East is fed by southerly flow at the surface and at the 850 mb level. Showers and thunderstorms produce floods and tornados from New England to the Southeast. A low pressure system in the upper Midwest is also fed by the southerly flow at both levels which causes severe weather to erupt there as well.

June 19, 2000
A low pressure system builds in the Dakotas and moves east into a warm, moist body of air in the Midwest. Thunderstorms are heaviest in the Midwest with isolated storms in the Southeast.

June 20, 2000
A surface low deepens in the Midwest as it moves to the northeast. Definitive wind and dew point gradients clearly mark the warm and the cold front boundaries. A squall line develops on the cold front and moves into the central Midwest.

July 5, 2000
A stationary front across the country touches off many pockets of showers and thunderstorms from the East coast to the central Plains. Temperatures on the south side of the front are in the 90`s to iniate the thunderstorms.

July 11, 2000
A small disturbance moves into the upper Midwest causing thunderstorms to develop in the Dakotas and move into Minnesota and Iowa. A stationary front from the Carolinas back west to Missouri also causes some thunderstorms to light off.

July 14, 2000
A low pressure system moves out of the Midwest and into the Ohio Valley where the state of Ohio gets hit hard with warnings for tornados, flooding and severe thunderstorms. Another, smaller surface low affects the Southeast.

July 16, 2000
This case is primarily an East coast and a Northeast event early in the evening as a low slides up the coast. In the late evening, a new area of severe weather pops up in the central Plains along a dew point gradient.

July 18, 2000
A cold front dives out of Canada and runs into a warmer, moister air mass. Thunderstorms develop from New England to the Midwest with tornados in Illinois and Missouri.

July 22, 2000
A low pressure system moves into the Southeast and stretches cold fronts to the northeast and west where thunderstorms develop along. Another disturbance in the central Rockies and central Plains causes a severe weather breakout in Kansas, Colorado and Nebraska.

Winter Cases


October 25, 1996
Large cyclone in northern Plains; blizzard in South Dakota; tornado outbreak in eastern South Dakota and Minnesota.

December 25, 1996
Frontogenesis case produced a 4-8" snow band in central Iowa as well as a narrow 8-10" snow band. Radar and theta-E data available.

February 3, 1997
Heavy snow in Iowa. Ice in southeast Iowa. Possible CSI. Models did a better job late.

November 10, 1998
Supercyclone heads towards Iowa and Minnesota with central pressure extremely low around 970 mb. Extreme wind, precip variations.

January 1, 1999
Cyclone develops in the Rockies and swings up from Texas towards Ohio Valley bringing ice to many southern states, heavy snow and blizzard condtions to Midwest and Great Lakes regions.

January 2, 1999
The cyclone from the previous day continues its trek to the Northeast, on the way dropping snow from Ohio Valley to Maine.

January 19, 2000
A low develops in the Rockies and moves from Wyoming to southwest Iowa giving central Iowa moderate snow fall amounts.

January 22, 2000
A good "ice storm" case for the Southeast. Warm air above the surface with below freezing temperatures at the surface for many southeastern states.

January 25, 2000
A very well developed "nor'easter" moves up the east coast dumping almost 2 feet of snow in the Carolinas and proceeding into New England with no signs of weakening.

February 17, 2000
A winter storm begins in the lee of the Rockies and drops snow on the foothills of Colorado. Some other snows in Nevada and Montana with freezing rain in Illinois.

February 18, 2000
A typical winter storm track from Texas to southeast Iowa bringing heavy snows to the Midwest and eventually swinging up to New England. Models did a good job.

December 11, 2000
A strong cyclone makes its way from the lee of the Rockies to the Ohio Valley. Large amounts of snow are dropped in the upper Midwest and the Great Lakes. High winds from the deepening low pressure kick up the fresh snow and cause near white-out conditions in some areas. Cold temperatures are also associated with this system.

December 18, 2000
A low pressure system pulls out of the Rockies and hits the upper Midwest with its second major snowstorm in less than a week. Heavy snow and cold temperatures are reported from the northern Plains to New York and Pennsylvania.

December 30, 2000
A surface low deepens as it enters the Ohio Valley and the Northeast. Heavy snow is dropped from the Great Lakes to the eastern seaboard.

March 22, 2001
A low pressure system moves from the mid-Atlantic region up the Northeast coast in typical nor`easter fashion. Large amounts of moisture are pulled in from the Atlantic Ocean resulting in heavy snow from Pennsylvania to Maine.
