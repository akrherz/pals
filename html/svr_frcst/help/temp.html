Surface Temperature Map Help Page

The surface temperature map shows not only surface temperature but also wind direction and speed.

A contoured surface temperature map
Surface Temperatures: Different surface temperatures (in degrees
fahrenheit) are shown by the different colors on this map. The closer the colors are to red, the warmer the temperatures are. The different contours are determined by temperature ranges of 5 degrees fahrenheit.

Wind Direction/Speed: The solid black stick like lines on the map represent the wind speed/direction at that location. The direction is determined by where the shorter black line extending from the stick is located.

Direction
Stick points to bottom of map = wind from the south
Stick points to left of map = wind from the west
Stick points to top of map = wind from the north
Stick points to right of map = wind from the east
Speed
Wind speed is determined by the number and length of lines
coming out of the end of the stick.
Short line = 5 mph (miles per hour)
Long line = 10 mph
Triangle = 50 mph

Helpful Questions and Anwers:

  • What does it mean when there is a sharp change in temperature? For example, temperatures in Nebraska are in the 50s while temperatures in Iowa are in the 70s.
  • When there is a sharp change in temperature, it generally indicates a front is in the area. In the above example, a cold front is probably pushing into Iowa from Nebraska. Remember fronts are a good lifting mechanism that cause storms to form.

  • What does it mean when the wind barbs are pointing towards each other?
  • When wind barbs are pointing towards each other, meteorologists say that convergence is occurring. This means that wind is blowing towards each other from opposite directions and it has no where to go but up. Upward motion is what causes clouds to form, and if there is strong enough upward motion, storms may form.
