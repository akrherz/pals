Upper Level Trough Help Page

A trough is a southward extension of relatively low heights, usually accompanied by cold air in the low and middle atmosphere. An upper level trough can be seen through a southward dip in the height lines in the upper atmosphere. When the 500 mb trough is in front of the surface low the surface low should weaken. When the 500 mb trough is behind the surface low the surface low should intensify.







In the 500 mb map above a trough dips down through the Rocky Mountains to a point in the northeastern portion of Colorado. The surface map shows the low to be in the Dakotas. This means that with the low pressure system ahead of the upper level trough the low pressure system will intensify.
