Wind Shear Help Page

Wind shear is the directional and speed change of wind with respect to height. This is a good variable to look at when predicting tornadic activity. If there is a lot of directional change with height, it means the atmosphere has a lot of spin (vorticity) and tornadic activity is possible. A good example of this would be if the wind direction at the surface was southerly, but at 500 mb the wind direction was westerly. A good example of wind speed differential would be about 30 mph.
