Explanation of Shear
 
    The animation below shows a cloud being tilted due to increasing wind speeds with height.  The stronger winds in the upper part of the atmosphere moves the cloud faster than the slower wind speeds nearer the surface. This wind shear causes precipitation to fall to the right of the updraft. This mechanism allows the updraft and the downdraft to coexist since the precipitation no longer falls on top of the updraft. This type of storm may continue to grow into a more intense thunderstorm.
