Dissipating Stage: All Downdrafts

     
    Important features on the animation below include:
    • Orange lines - Isotherms (equal lines of temperature).
    • Numbers on the left hand side - Height of atmosphere in kilometer.
    • Number on the right hand side - Height of atmosphere in pressure.
    • Loading time: 1 minute for 28.8 modem.
    		 During this stage the cumulus cloud begins to dissipate due to the fact that the downdrafts have cut off the updrafts. The system dies as a result.  Precipitation comes to an end as the cloud begins to evaporate. 
     
     
   This portion of the thunderstorm life cycle is the dissipating stage.  The falling precipitation continues to strengthen the downdraft while quenching the updrafts.  This process cuts off the energy source of the thunderstorm. In time the precipitation weakens as the cloud begins to evaporate. Evaporation is a cooling process which also enhances the strength of the downdraft by making the air more dense (colder). This can be seen on the animation as the isotherms (equal lines of temperature) bend downward in the center of the cloud. By the end of this stage a major section of the cloud has evaporated and spread out in the upper atmosphere. Precipitation is no longer being produced and all that remains of the thunderstorm is a layer of high clouds (Return to Quick Time Movie).
