Expert Explanation

      The correct response is (C) wind speeds increase with height.  An expert would say that severe thunderstorms can develop more readily when the atmosphere experiences increasing wind speeds with respect to height.  This allows the downdraft in the system to be tilted to the side of the updraft.  With this vertical setup the downdraft doesn't quench the updraft.  Therefore increasing the thunderstorms lifetime and intensity.
