Stable Weather Conditions

        This balloon sounding portrays an atmosphere that is stable.  Thunderstorms are very unlikely in this situation.  A stable atmosphere is present when the temperature of a lifted parcel (bubble of air) is less than the surrounding air temperature.  This can be seen on the balloon sounding.  The air parcel path is represented by the yellow line while the actual air temperature is represented by the white line farthest to the right on the sounding.  This sounding shows the parcel temperature is colder than the actual air temperature at any level, therefore its density is greater than the surrounding air.  These conditions will cause the lifted parcel to sink.
