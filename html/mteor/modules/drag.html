Drag

     This sections explores the role that drag plays in the life cycle of a thunderstorm.  Drag is a frictional force that is exerted on and by moving objects.  For our discussion we are concerned with the effect drag has on both the raindrops and the surrounding air.  As rain begins to fall the air surrounding the raindrops act to slow the rate of decent.  In this process the surrounding air is pulled (dragged) downward by the falling raindrops.  This results in downward motion within the thunderstorm which is called a downdraft.
