Expert Explanation

         Severe thunderstorms result when the atmosphere experiences increasing wind speed with respect to height.  This allows the downdraft in the system to be tilted to the side of the updraft.  With this veritical setup the downdraft doesn't quench the updraft.  This allows for further strengthing and development.  Therefore the potential increases for these systems to produce hail, strong winds, flash floods, downdrafts, and even tornadoes.
