Mature Stage: Both Updrafts And Downdrafts

    Important features on the animation below include:

    • Orange lines - Isotherms (equal lines of temperature).
    • Numbers on the left hand side - Height of atmosphere in kilometer.
    • Number on the right hand side - Height of atmosphere in pressure.
    • Loading time: 1 minute for 28.8 modem.
    		 During this stage the cumulus cloud has reached its maximum growth in the vertical due to the cloud reaching the top of tropausphere.  At the surface the precipitation has begun. Incresed downdrafts are the result. 
     
     
   This is the second stage a thunderstorm goes through during development.    Precipitation has began falling from the base of the cloud.  Updrafts dominate the beginning of this stage as the thunderstorm reaches its maximun potential in growth and intensity. The cloud in the upper part of the animation can be seen spreading out in the horizontal direction. This occured due to fact that the cloud has bumped up against the tropopause (upper part of troposphere). The atmosphere in this area is very stable which quenches further development in the vertical direction (Return to Quick Time Movie).
