An expert would say that when the air inside a parcel (bubble of air) is colder than the surrounding air temperature the parcel is negatively buoyant.  This condition leads to descending motion in the atmosphere.  Although this is not the only factor that leads to descending motion. Also falling precipitation drags air downward enhancing the motion.  These descending motions combine and eventually cut off the updraft by occupying the same vertical region, therefore the system dies.
