Let’s consider why it is colder at the top of mountains.

    1. If the earth had no atmosphere the mean temperature at the surface would be about 250K (~9F).
    2. When we add the atmosphere the mean temperature at the surface increases to about 283K (~50F). The atmosphere radiates IR thus it contributes energy to the surface.
    3. At the top of a mountain there is less atmosphere above to radiate IR so the temperature is lower than at the surface.
    4. Death Valley is further below sea level than anywhere else in the U.S. Guess where the record hot temperatures are found? Why?
