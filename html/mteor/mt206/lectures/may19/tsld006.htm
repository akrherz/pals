Dew Point

    The air can keep cooling until it reaches the dew point.
    The dew point is the temperature at which the water vapor
    in the air condenses. When the air cools to the dew point,
    energy used to keep the water molecules in a gaseous
    state is released as heat which opposes the cooling.
    Generally, when conditions are favorable for cooling
    in the real world, the temperature drops to within a few
    degrees of the dew point.
